### 4.1 — Medicare FFS + Medigap: anesthesia group filed 17 months late → patient sent to collections for $3,000
- **Insurance:** Traditional Medicare Part B + Humana Medigap; MSN is the adjudication doc. Medicare denied (timely-filing), so Medigap auto-denied (it follows Medicare). **Engine-relevant? Yes** — root cause is a provider-side filing-timing error, and the lever is citing the federal billing rule, not appealing the patient's own claim.
- **Money:** collections demand **~$3,000**; Medicare/Medigap paid **$0**; patient legally owed **$0** (under assignment + 42 CFR §424.44, a provider who misses the 12-month window **cannot bill the beneficiary**).
- **What happened:** NAPA (PE-owned staffing co.) submitted ~17 months post-op; Medicare denied; NAPA sent it to collections anyway. Months of the wife's calls did nothing → **KFF story → CMS confirmed on the record the provider can't bill** → demand neutralized.
- **Levers:** read the MSN denial code ("timely filing"); **FDCPA dispute to the collector within 30 days** citing §424.44; CMS / 1-800-Medicare complaint; state AG; SHIP. No FFS appeal needed (it's a debt rebuttal, not a denial of his own claim). Press decisive.
- **Signal:** **a provider billing error becomes the patient's problem via collections**; the MSN denial code is itself the defense; **Medigap follows Medicare** (no Medicare pay = no Medigap pay); "provider's claim denied" ≠ "patient owes."
- **Source:** [KFF/NPR, Jul 27 2023](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/anesthesiologists-billed-medicare-late-collections-charges/)

### 4.2 — Medicare FFS: hospital switched inpatient→observation at discharge → $9,145 SNF bill (Alexander v. Azar)
- **Insurance:** Traditional Medicare Part A+B; MSN + hospital Condition Code 44 / MOON notice. Without a qualifying **3-day inpatient** stay, the Part A SNF benefit never triggers → patient owes 100% as outpatient. **Engine-relevant? No** (status-classification / denial).
- **Money:** patient paid **$9,145** out-of-pocket for the SNF stay (would've been ~$0 days 1–20 under Part A). Retroactive reimbursement now possible for the class (stays back to Jan 1 2009).
- **What happened:** at the time (2016) there was **no way to appeal** the switch — the entire point of the class action. Kanefsky (94) became a named plaintiff; **2020 ruling (aff'd 2022) created a due-process appeal right**; CMS final rule (Oct 2024, eff. 2025) implements both expedited during-stay and retrospective appeals.
- **Levers:** **expedited BFCC-QIO appeal before discharge** (1-business-day decision) on the MOON notice; post-discharge Part A redetermination (CMS-20033, **120 days** from MSN) citing the original *physician* inpatient order; 5-level FFS ladder; **retroactive class claims** via Center for Medicare Advocacy. Key test: did a *physician* order inpatient and a hospital UR committee reverse it? (yes → appeal right).
- **Signal:** **observation-status switches silently void the SNF benefit**; a structural denial fixed only by **years of class litigation**; the decisive fact is *who* reclassified (physician vs. UR committee).
- **Source:** [KFF, Mar 27 2020](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/federal-judge-rules-medicare-patients-can-challenge-observation-care-status/) · [CMS, Alexander v. Azar](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/appeals-grievances/original-medicare-appeals/hospital-appeals-change-inpatient-status-alexander-v-azar)

### 4.3 — Medicare Advantage: AI algorithm (nH Predict) cut SNF coverage over physicians' objections (Lokken/Tetzloff)
- **Insurance:** Medicare Advantage (UnitedHealthcare); EOB is the adjudication doc; **naviHealth** subsidiary ran continued-stay reviews; SNFs in-network (this is a **continued-stay authorization** denial, not admission/OON). MA enrollees can't hold Medigap. **Engine-relevant? No** (algorithmic denial / appeal).
- **Money:** **Lokken (91)** paid ~**$150,000**; **Tetzloff (74)** paid ~**$70,000** — both out-of-pocket after UHC cut coverage; **both died** before any recovery. (OIG Jun 2026: **97%** of appealed naviHealth SNF denials were overturned.)
- **What happened:** UHC/naviHealth paid ~19–20 days then denied despite documented medical necessity; Tetzloff's wife **appealed and won a reversal** → UHC paid to day 40 → **re-denied**. Class action (*Lokken v. UnitedHealth*, D. Minn., filed Nov 2023; alleges nH Predict "90% error rate") **survived a motion to dismiss (Feb 2025)**, now in discovery.
- **Levers:** demand a written organization determination with a specific reason code; **expedited IREO (Maximus) reconsideration (72h)** for ongoing termination; **the treating physician's written medical-necessity statement is the decisive exhibit**; peer-to-peer; ALJ (≥~$180) → MAC → federal court; cite **CMS 2023/2024 guidance barring algorithmic UM that overrides individualized determinations**.
- **Signal:** **MA continued-stay denials by algorithm are a major pattern**; **internal appeals can win then get re-denied**; the 97% overturn-on-appeal stat means **the agent must drive every denial up the ladder**; engine never touches it.
- **Source:** [STAT, Nov 14 2023](https://www.statnews.com/2023/11/14/unitedhealth-class-action-lawsuit-algorithm-medicare-advantage/) · [OIG, Jun 2026](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2026/medicare-advantage-organizations-overturned-nearly-all-appealed-prior-authorization-denials-for-skilled-nursing-facility-admission-raising-concerns-about-initial-denials/)

### 4.4 — Medicare Advantage: in-network *directory* error (vision network ≠ medical network), $340; won via appeal + press
- **Insurance:** MA (UnitedHealthcare, 2025); EOB + denial letter ("no out-of-network benefits"). UHC keeps **separate vision and medical networks**; the clinic was in-network for *vision* only, but glaucoma monitoring is *medical* → adjudicated OON. **Engine-relevant? No** (directory error + denial).
- **Money:** billed **~$340**; UHC paid $0 → patient owed ~$340 → **$0** after a "good-faith exception."
- **What happened:** Tuszynski (70) **screenshot-less** had checked the website (green "in-network" check) pre-visit; UHC denied; she appealed + called 1-800-Medicare/CMS; **KFF inquiry → UHC made a one-time "good faith" exception** (no directory fix, future visits still uncovered). She left MA for Original Medicare + Medigap for 2026.
- **Levers:** **screenshot the directory listing with a timestamp** (primary exhibit); MA internal appeal (**60 days**) citing 42 CFR §422.111(b)(3) directory-accuracy duty + reasonable reliance; CMS + state-DOI complaints (CMS can fine for directory errors); **network-adequacy** argument (cover OON at in-network rates if no adequate in-network option); consider switching plans at AEP (Oct 15–Dec 7).
- **Signal:** **MA bifurcated vision/medical networks routinely mislead patients**; the plan's own directory is the lever; wins come as **one-off exceptions, not fixes** → "leave the plan" is sometimes the real remedy.
- **Source:** [KFF, Jan 30 2026](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/medicare-advantage-eye-care-wisconsin-bill-of-the-month-january-2026/)

### 4.5 — Medicare Advantage: naviHealth algorithm cut SNF on day 17; reversed in federal court a year+ later — patient died, savings gone (Frances Walter)
- **Insurance:** MA (Security Health Plan of WI, ~2019); EOB + continued-stay determination + NOMNC; naviHealth UM; in-network SNF; continued-stay termination. **Engine-relevant? No** (algorithmic denial / appeal).
- **Money:** coverage cut **day 17** (days 1–20 should be $0 cost-share); judge later said she was owed **"thousands for 3+ weeks"**; during the fight she **spent down her life savings and enrolled in Medicaid**.
- **What happened:** naviHealth's algorithm predicted readiness at **16.6 days** despite notes that she (85, shattered shoulder, pain-med allergy) couldn't dress/toilet/walk; she fought **>1 year** up to a **federal judge who ruled the insurer's call "at best, speculative"** and reversed — but she **died before the STAT story ran (Mar 2023)**. Directly informed CMS's algorithm-UM restrictions.
- **Levers:** act on **day 1 of termination** — written org determination; **QIO expedited appeal on the NOMNC before the effective date** (1-business-day decision); IREO expedited (72h); physician medical-necessity statement; peer-to-peer; ALJ→MAC→court; **don't voluntarily spend down** — get SHIP/elder-law help; cite CMS algorithm-UM guidance.
- **Signal:** **"won on paper ≠ made whole"** in the extreme — a technical legal win after death, savings already gone; **speed is everything** (NOMNC/QIO clock); same naviHealth pattern as 4.3.
- **Source:** [STAT, Mar 13 2023](https://www.statnews.com/2023/03/13/medicare-advantage-plans-denial-artificial-intelligence/) · [KFF, feds rein in predictive software](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/biden-administration-software-algorithms-medicare-advantage/)

---
**Segment signals:** (1) **Two distinct Medicare worlds.** *FFS+Medigap:* billing-rule defenses (timely-filing → provider can't bill; observation-status → SNF benefit voided), MSN is the evidence, Medigap follows Medicare. *Advantage:* the dominant problem is **utilization-management denials, increasingly by algorithm (naviHealth/nH Predict)**, with EOB-driven appeals. (2) **The Medicare appeal ladder is a core tool the agent must drive:** FFS = redetermination→QIC→ALJ→MAC→court (120-day first clock); MA = org determination→IREO (Maximus)→ALJ→MAC→court (60-day clocks), with **expedited/QIO/NOMNC tracks (72h / 1-business-day) that are decisive for ongoing care**. (3) **The treating physician's written medical-necessity statement is the recurring decisive exhibit** for MA denials; **OIG 97% overturn-on-appeal** means most denials shouldn't stand — so *never let one go unappealed*. (4) **"Won on paper ≠ made whole"** recurs hard (4.2, 4.3, 4.5: years of litigation, deaths, spent-down savings) → **speed + the deadline clocks are the product's job**. (5) **Structural/legal-landscape shifts matter** (Alexander v. Azar appeal right, CMS algorithm-UM rules) → versioned-knowledge discipline. (6) Engine relevance is **low** here (only 4.1, and only as a rule-citation), reinforcing engine-as-minority-tool.
