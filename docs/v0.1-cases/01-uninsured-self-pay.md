# V0.1 cases — Segment 1: Uninsured / self-pay

> **Status: INPUT (V0.1).** 5 real, cited cases. Template v2.1. Index: [../v0.1-cases.md](../v0.1-cases.md). _2026-06-17._

### 1.1 — Uninsured veteran, $80,232 for two emergency appendix surgeries; charity care denied on a technicality
- **Insurance:** Uninsured, self-pay; no EOB; no GFE (emergent, pre-NSA). **Engine-relevant? Partial** (a complication-surgery + chargemaster-vs-market angle).
- **Money:** billed **$80,232** ($35,906 + $44,326) → after grievance (2nd surgery written off) + discounts, **~$22,304** owed.
- **What happened:** denied hospital charity care AND Colorado CICP because one month's **overtime** put him ~$200 over the income cutoff. A formal **grievance** (incl. a **quality-of-care** argument that the 2nd surgery was a preventable complication) got the $44k written off; a $12k lump-sum offer was refused; ended in payment-plan talks. Press = modest.
- **Levers:** re-apply for 501(r)/CICP using **representative (non-overtime) income**; **quality-of-care grievance**; benchmark vs Medicare; lump-sum/hardship; payment plan.
- **Signal:** charity care denied on a **technicality** (one outlier income month); a **quality/complication** argument can void a charge.
- **Source:** [KFF/NPR, Aug 25 2020](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/bill-of-the-month-veteran-appendectomy-complication-long-battle-over-bill/)

### 1.2 — Uninsured woman, GFE $50,560 → bill $97,998 (the textbook GFE→PPDR trigger)
- **Insurance:** Uninsured, self-pay; **a Good Faith Estimate was issued** (NSA). **Engine-relevant? Yes** (GFE-vs-actual + itemized supply/implant review).
- **Money:** GFE **$50,560**; billed **$97,998** (**$47,438 over**, far past the $400 PPDR threshold); self-pay discount → **$78,398.40** owed.
- **What happened:** hospital blamed "complications"; **CMS itself called her to explain her PPDR rights**; she plans to file. Unresolved.
- **Levers:** **PPDR (primary)** — file within **120 days**, $25 fee; "complications" boilerplate doesn't categorically void the GFE; benchmark vs Medicare; itemized review of $50k implants; charity care; negotiate with PPDR as leverage.
- **Signal:** GFE breaches are real and **massively underused**; the best-documented PPDR case we found. Ask: one consolidated GFE or separate per provider?
- **Source:** [KFF, Sep 24 2025](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/broken-arm-uninsured-surprise-bill-of-the-month-september/)

### 1.3 — Uninsured student: hospital grants 100% charity care, but the independent physician groups bill $5k+ (the 501(r) loophole) ⟵ multi-biller
- **Insurance:** Uninsured, self-pay; no GFE (emergent). **Engine-relevant? Partial** (structural, not coding).
- **Money:** ~**$100,000** hospital facility bills **100% forgiven** under 501(r); but **EM + anesthesia + radiology groups billed $5,000+**, NOT covered by the hospital's policy.
- **What happened:** she paid the physician bills via payment plans + scholarship/stimulus. Colorado later passed a law requiring contracted physician groups to match the hospital's assistance — too late for her.
- **Levers:** apply to **each physician group's own** hardship/charity program; hardship lump-sum; check the new state law; FDCPA if any went to collections; benchmark.
- **Signal (important):** **501(r) covers the hospital *entity*, not contracted physician groups** → one episode = multiple billers with **different charity policies & statuses.** Direct validation of our **case → multiple-bills** model.
- **Source:** [KFF/CBS, Sep 25 2025](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/hospital-charity-care-loopholes-needy-patients-pay/)

### 1.4 — Uninsured ER patient sued for $12,019; debt snowballs to $33,000 over 12 years; forgiven only after investigative press
- **Insurance:** Uninsured, self-pay; income (~$12k/yr) should have qualified for **100% charity care**, never offered. **Engine-relevant? No** (charity-care denial + collections/legal).
- **Money:** billed **$12,019** → judgment $16k+ → **$33,000** by 2019 (interest applied 7×, **wages garnished 15×**) → **100% forgiven**.
- **What happened:** nonprofit hospital (Methodist Le Bonheur) **sued instead of screening** for charity care; debt forgiven only after an **MLK50/ProPublica investigation** (6,500+ patients' debts erased). 12 years of garnishment, no compensation.
- **Levers:** demand 501(r) charity care + **IRS Form 13909** to report the violation; **FDCPA** validation; challenge interest/fees/judgment (improper notice); state AG; **investigative press** (worked here).
- **Signal:** charity-eligible patients get **sued not screened**; debt snowballs via interest/fees; **investigative press is a real, decisive lever** (reinforces a productized press play). New tool: **Form 13909.**
- **Source:** [ProPublica/MLK50, Jun 27 2019](https://www.propublica.org/article/methodist-le-bonheur-healthcare-sues-poor-medical-debt) · [forgiveness update](https://mlk50.com/we-reported-on-methodist-le-bonheur-suing-patients-it-erased-the-debts-of-more-than-6500-patients-ade13e8e2e6d)

### 1.5 — The cash-price paradox: price tool said ~$1,400; real self-pay $8k–$11.5k; chargemaster $17,979 (insured patient, documents the self-pay mechanism)
- **Insurance:** *Insured* (Cigna HDHP) — included because it documents the **cash/chargemaster price mechanism** for self-pay. **Engine-relevant? Yes** (price-transparency + benchmark).
- **Money:** chargemaster **$17,979**; negotiated **$8,424**; insurer paid $3,254; owed **$5,169** → **36% prompt-pay discount** (~$3,308). Published "uninsured" price **~$1,400** (hospital called it a "glitch"); competitor cash **~$2,100**.
- **Levers:** **benchmark vs competitor + Medicare**; **prompt-pay/self-pay discount** (ask explicitly); **price-transparency complaint** (45 CFR Part 180 — published price was wrong); charity care; itemized review.
- **Signal:** hospital **price tools are unreliable**; the self-pay "discount" is still multiples of competitors; prompt-pay discount + benchmark are reliable self-pay levers.
- **Source:** [KFF/NPR, Aug 23 2022](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/bill-of-the-month-breast-biopsy-with-insurance/)

---
**Segment signals:** (1) **Charity care/501(r) is high-value but trap-laden:** technicality denials (income month), the **physician-group loophole** (multi-biller), and eligible-but-*sued*. (2) **GFE→PPDR** is a real, underused lever; "complications" don't void it. (3) New levers/tools: **IRS Form 13909** (report 501(r) violations), **prompt-pay/cash-price + competitor benchmark**, **price-transparency complaints**. (4) **Investigative press** decisively resolved the most egregious case. (5) Multi-biller episodes (one event, many billers, different policies/statuses) keep recurring → confirms the **case → bill(s)** model.
