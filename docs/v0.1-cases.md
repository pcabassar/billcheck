## Case 01 — "One surgery, billed as two" (duplicate / unbundled facility fee · insured · in-network)

1. **Situation + who.** Jamie Holmes (NW Washington), **insured**, had a **pre-authorized tubal ligation** (2019) at an **in-network** facility — Pacific Rim Outpatient Surgery Center, Bellingham WA. Mid-operation the surgeon also cauterized early endometriosis tissue as a precaution — **one operative session.** The surgery center billed it as **two separate operations.**
2. **Document timeline.** Itemized facility bill with **two line items** ($4,810 each) → insurer adjudication/EOB → patient **balance bill** → debt **sold to a collection agency** → **lawsuit**. (No itemized-vs-EOB reconciliation was ever done for her — that's the gap.)
3. **The numbers.** Billed **$9,620** (2 × $4,810). Insurer paid **$1,262**; after contractual adjustment patient billed **$2,605**; collections **lawsuit $3,792.19** (w/ interest & fees). Disputed core: **one of the two $4,810 facility fees** — she had one operation, not two.
4. **Her question/goal.** "I had **one** surgery — why am I billed for two?" → after being sued: "How do I not pay for an operation that didn't happen, without wrecking my credit?"
5. **What happened / the right help.** She **refused to pay** and **defended the suit**; as of the Aug-2024 story it was **unresolved/ongoing** (KFF noted that contesting can force a judge to deny summary judgment → trial → the collector may drop it). *Right help:* pull the **itemized bill + operative note**, show one operative session ⇒ the second facility fee is a **duplicate/unbundled charge**; dispute with facility/insurer **before** collections; once in collections, **FDCPA validation**; defend the suit with the one-surgery evidence. **Engine-relevant** (duplicate / NCCI-unbundling).
6. **Maps-to-our-model.** One **case** (the surgery episode) → one **bill** (surgery-center charge) → **documents:** itemized bill (2 lines), EOB, collection notice, lawsuit summons. *Bill lifecycle:* should have run New → Gathering (itemized + op note) → Reviewed (duplicate finding) → Acting (dispute); **reality skipped straight to collections/lawsuit.** *Agent needs:* the itemized bill + operative note (or her account "one surgery"); the **engine's duplicate/unbundling check**; knowledge of **FDCPA validation**, **defending a collections suit**, **statute of limitations**; dispute-letter + court-response artifacts. *Clocks:* FDCPA 30-day; lawsuit answer deadline; SOL.
7. **Source.** KFF Health News / NPR "Bill of the Month," Aug 19–21 2024 — [KFF](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/bill-of-the-month-one-surgery-charged-for-two-collections-lawsuit/) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2024/08/19/nx-s1-5072975/she-was-on-the-surgical-table-just-once-but-was-billed-for-two-operations) · [Seattle Times](https://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/health/wa-woman-had-1-surgery-but-was-billed-for-2-she-refused-to-pay/).

## Case 02 — "Out-of-network ground ambulance after a crash" (No Surprises Act gap · insured · advice/lever, engine-irrelevant)

1. **Situation + who.** Peggy Dula (St. Charles, IL), **insured**, least-injured of 3 siblings whose car was struck by a pickup (Sept 2021). The three rode **three different fire-district ambulances**; hers was **out-of-network**.
2. **Document timeline.** Ambulance invoice → insurer paid "**reasonable and customary**," leaving a **balance bill** → ~a year of disputing → **collections** (Wakefield & Associates).
3. **The numbers.** Charge **$3,606** ($30/mile vs. siblings' $10/mile; >2× siblings for the same medical services). Patient owed **~$2,711** after the insurer's R&C payment.
4. **Her question/goal.** "Why is my bill **3× my siblings'** for the same ride — and why am I stuck with it when I had no choice of ambulance?"
5. **What happened / the right help.** **No clean lever existed** — the **No Surprises Act explicitly excludes ground ambulance**, so the balance bill was legal. She fought ~a year; it went to collections; it was **only resolved after CBS Mornings/NPR/KHN coverage** — the fire district pulled it from collections and **zeroed the balance** (Sept 2022). *Realistic product help (absent press):* explain the NSA gap honestly, **negotiate** with the fire district, check **Illinois ambulance-billing law**, **appeal the insurer's R&C**, handle **collections via FDCPA**. **Engine does not apply** — no coding error; the charge is "valid," just unfair.
6. **Maps-to-our-model.** One **case** → one **bill** (ambulance charge) → **documents:** invoice, EOB, collection notice. *Bill lifecycle:* New → Gathering → Reviewed ("you're not protected; here are your options") → Acting (negotiate/appeal). *Agent needs:* knowledge of the **NSA ground-ambulance exclusion**, **state ambulance laws**, **R&C appeals**, **negotiation scripts**, **FDCPA**; tools = knowledge-base lookup + a negotiation/appeal artifact — **no engine run.** *Clocks:* insurer-appeal window; FDCPA. **Hard truth surfaced:** the real resolution came from **media leverage a product can't scale** — so V0.1's honest value is *orientation + the best available lever + persistence*, not a guaranteed win.
7. **Source.** KFF / NPR / CBS "Bill of the Month," Jul 2022 + Sept 2022 update — [KFF (collections)](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/ambulance-balance-bill-surprise-car-crash/) · [KFF ($2,700 pulled back)](https://kffhealthnews.org/health-care-costs/ambulance-bill-collections-surprise-wreck/) · [Chicago Sun-Times](https://chicago.suntimes.com/2022/9/29/23376944/medical-bills-peggy-dula-st-charles-cbs-mornings-npr-pingree-grove-countryside-fire-protection).

## Template stress-test notes → proposed v2

What the two cases strained, and the fixes I'd make:
1. **"The numbers" wants structure.** Both have 4–5 distinct figures. Make it a tiny ledger: **billed / insurer-allowed / insurer-paid / patient-owed / in-collections / disputed-amount.**
2. **Split field 5 in two.** "What *actually happened*" (faithful — incl. dead-ends, time, and *how it really resolved*, e.g. "only via press") is not the same as "the **ideal play / lever**" (what billcheck *should* do). Dula resolved via media — not a scalable product capability. Keep reality and design-target separate.
3. **Add "what they'd already tried."** (Holmes had refused to pay, then was sued; Dula fought ~a year.) Shapes where the agent picks up.
4. **Split "maps-to-our-model" into three:** (a) data shape (case/bill/documents), (b) bill-lifecycle path *incl. where reality skipped/jumped*, (c) **what the agent needs** (knowledge · tools · questions).
5. **Add an "engine-relevant?" tag.** Case 01 = yes (duplicate/unbundling); Case 02 = no (advice-only). *Already 1 of 2 is engine-irrelevant — validates "engine is one tool, mostly advice."*
6. **Add "clocks/deadlines in play."** Collections/FDCPA, lawsuit-answer, SOL, appeal windows recur.

### Cross-case signals (early, from n=2)
- **The engine is a minority tool.** 1 of 2 cases has no coding error at all. Reinforces the advisor-+-tools framing.
- **Collections/legal is a recurring *late* stage, not an edge case.** Both ended in collections; one in a lawsuit. "Bill in collections / being sued" must be first-class.
- **Evidence often lives in the *records*, not just the bill.** Holmes' proof is "one operative session" (the operative note) — the patient's *account of what happened* is itself evidence.
- **Honest-value limit.** Where no lever exists, V0.1 can't promise a win — its value is orientation + best-available lever + lowering activation energy. (Ties to the honesty wedge.)
