### 6.1 — Sued over hospital bills she never received; 7% interest snowball (resolved via press + $500 settlement)
- **Insurance:** Uninsured at service; bills not coding-erroneous, but **never delivered** (unstable address). **Engine-relevant? No** (collections/lawsuit/SOL).
- **Money:** billed **$11,590** → judgment **$11,749 + 7%/yr** → ~$14,465; she'd paid ~$5,200 over 5 yrs. Closed for an extra **$500** after press.
- **What happened:** Ballad Health (original creditor) **sued without her ever seeing a bill**; she found out when served. **KFF story → quick $500 settlement, "paid in full."**
- **Levers:** **motion to vacate default** (improper service; TN 30-day window — expired); **501(r) notify-before-ECA**; retroactive charity care; **negotiate judgment payoff** (collectors take 20–50%); SOL/judgment-renewal awareness; press.
- **Signal:** never-notified → sued → interest snowball; **negotiating a lump-sum judgment payoff** works; press decisive. (FDCPA's 30-day validation does **not** apply to the hospital as *original creditor*.)
- **Source:** [KFF/NPR, Jun 2023](https://kffhealthnews.org/courts/bill-of-the-month-june-2023-undeliverable-unreceived-hospital-bills-medical-debt/)

### 6.2 — Methodist Le Bonheur: $12k → $33k, 15 garnishments, erased only after ProPublica — *= case 1.4 (cross-ref; count once)*
- Adds levers: **motion to vacate default**, **IRS Form 13909** (501(r) violation), **garnishment limits** (25% cap), **bankruptcy** (Ch.7 discharges the judgment) as last resort.
- **Signal:** charity-eligible patients sued not screened; **investigative press** decisive; bankruptcy is a real floor.
- **Source:** [ProPublica/MLK50, Jul 2019](https://www.propublica.org/article/methodist-le-bonheur-healthcare-sues-poor-medical-debt)

### 6.3 — INSURED patient sued by a stealth third-party collector despite a **$0 balance** on his portal
- **Insurance:** Commercial employer plan; **portal showed $0** yet sued → likely **payment-posting / adjudication error**. Debt "assigned" to **Credit Service Company** (3rd-party) suing in its own name. **Engine-relevant? Maybe** (insurer paid but not posted).
- **Money:** Mayer's amount not documented; CSC filed **12,121 suits** ($34.3M principal), mostly **default judgments**. (Another patient: $24k for a 90-min ER visit.)
- **What happened:** patients sued without UCHealth's name on filings; **CO HB1380 (2024) now requires the hospital's name**; class action ongoing. Mayer's case unresolved.
- **Levers:** **FDCPA 30-day validation** (3rd-party collector → applies); **ANSWER the summons (21 days in CO) — never default**; **pull the EOB** to prove insurer paid; CO **debt-buyer standing** challenge; CO-FDCPA (covers original creditors too); negotiate.
- **Signal (big):** **default judgment is THE trap → answer the summons**; **third-party vs original creditor** decides FDCPA; **payment-posting errors cause wrongful suits** → the EOB is the proof.
- **Source:** [Colorado Sun, Feb 2024](https://coloradosun.com/2024/02/19/uchealth-debt-collectors/) · [9NEWS, Dec 2024](https://www.9news.com/article/news/investigations/uchealth-sues-patients-daily-and-some-have-no-idea-why/73-26511c7b-2646-4d99-bca8-754ad7afa0c5)

### 6.4 — Prior-auth denial of FDA-cleared cancer treatment, denied 4×, reversed via press — **too late; patient died**
- **Insurance:** WV PEIA (state employee plan, ERISA-exempt) admin by **UnitedHealthcare**. Treatment (histotripsy, ~$50k) deemed **"experimental / not medically necessary."** **Engine-relevant? No** (coverage determination).
- **Money:** treatment never delivered ($0 billed); the cost was the **delay**.
- **What happened:** every internal appeal + a PEIA-hired reviewer **denied**; reversed **only after KFF/NBC asked questions** — but he was then too sick; **died at 58.** His death drove WV prior-auth reform.
- **Levers:** **expedited internal appeal (72h)** for urgent/life-threatening (was this invoked?); **true external independent review**; **peer-to-peer** (oncologist ↔ medical director); **emergency injunction/TRO**; state insurance commissioner; press.
- **Signal:** **denials kill via delay**; **expedited review + external review + peer-to-peer** are the real levers; **"won on paper ≠ outcome"**; press can come **too late** → speed matters.
- **Source:** [KFF, Nov 2025](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/prior-authorization-denials-cancer-treatment-west-virginia-death/)

### 6.5 — IVIG denied for 2 years ($90k out-of-pocket); **external review overturned the denial** — but prospective only
- **Insurance:** Commercial Anthem (VA), employer plan → COBRA. IVIG ~$10k/infusion deemed **"not medically necessary"** for small-fiber neuropathy. **Engine-relevant? No** (coverage determination).
- **Money:** paid **~$90,000 out of pocket** during denial; **external review (VA Bureau of Insurance) overturned** Anthem → authorized **going forward**; **$90k not reimbursed**; COBRA expiring → restart risk.
- **Levers:** **external independent review (decisive)**; **expedited appeal**; **retroactive reimbursement claim** for amounts paid during wrongful denial (ERISA §502/state); peer-to-peer; employer/HR; **pre-auth with the new insurer before COBRA lapses**.
- **Signal:** **external review WINS where internal appeals fail** — but **winning ≠ made whole** (push timeliness + retroactive reimbursement + coverage-transition planning).
- **Source:** [KFF, Mar 2026](https://kffhealthnews.org/news/article/prior-authorization-insurer-pledge-awaiting-reforms-patients-families-bills/)

---
**Segment signals:** (1) **Denial pathway: internal appeal (often FAILS) → EXTERNAL independent review (often WINS).** Under-used; the agent must drive to external review + **expedited (72h)** for urgent + **peer-to-peer**. (2) **Collections rules:** **answer the summons — never default** (default judgment is the trap); **third-party collector → FDCPA validation**; **negotiate lump-sum payoff**; **vacate default** for improper service; **bankruptcy** as a real floor. (3) **Payment-posting/insurer errors cause wrongful suits → pull the EOB.** (4) **Investigative press is repeatedly decisive — but can arrive too late, and even a win may not make you whole** (push speed + retroactive reimbursement). (5) **The legal landscape shifts** (CO HB1380, WV prior-auth reform) → versioned-knowledge discipline matters.
