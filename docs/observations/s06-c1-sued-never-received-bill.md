# Observation — s06-c1: Sued over hospital bills she never received; 7% interest snowball

**Case:** uninsured at service, TN; default judgment after service-by-mail to an old address came back undeliverable; ~$5k paid but not credited ([source case 06](../initial-research/cases/06-collections-credit-denials.md), case 6.1)
**Input:** "I just got served… never even got a bill… judgment for almost twelve thousand plus 7% a year… I'd paid about five grand" + civil-summons/default-judgment PDF (billed $11,590 → judgment $11,749 @ 7%, service "returned undeliverable").
**Transcript:** [_raw/s06-c1-sued-never-received-bill.md](_raw/s06-c1-sued-never-received-bill.md)

## What happened
- Led with **the clock**: identified the **default judgment** and the **defective service** ("returned undeliverable," right on the doc) as the basis for a **motion to set aside / vacate**, and flagged it as time-sensitive.
- Correctly surfaced that the deadline to challenge a default for bad service often **runs from when she found out**, not the judgment date — and asked the unlocking question (**what date were you served?**).
- Caught that the **~$5k in payments may not have been credited** (judgment is for the full principal) — a documented lever in the real case.
- Named **retroactive charity care** (Ballad is nonprofit), pointed her to **Legal Aid of East Tennessee** + the **Clerk** + a **fee waiver / pauper's oath**, and told her **not to pay or acknowledge** anything yet.
