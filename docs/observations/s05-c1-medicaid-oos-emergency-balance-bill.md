# Observation — s05-c1: Florida Medicaid patient balance-billed $77,574 for an out-of-state heart attack

**Case:** [05-medicaid-dual-cob.md](../initial-research/cases/05-medicaid-dual-cob.md) §5.1 — FL Medicaid (only payer); out-of-state hospital never filed a claim and balance-billed the patient, which is barred.
**Input:** "I'm on Florida Medicaid… had a heart attack in South Dakota… they keep billing me over seventy thousand. Do I really owe this?" + Monument Health statement PDF ($77,574.44, insurance $0, "insurance on file: NONE", "not enrolled with Florida Medicaid").
**Transcript:** [_raw/s05-c1-medicaid-oos-emergency-balance-bill.md](_raw/s05-c1-medicaid-oos-emergency-balance-bill.md)

## What happened
- Opened with **"Don't sign that payment plan… you owe little or nothing"** — led with the protection, not the balance.
- Read the two load-bearing doc lines correctly: "insurance on file: NONE" (no claim ever filed) and "not enrolled with Florida Medicaid" (the hospital's excuse), and explained the difference between *not enrolled* and *you owe this*.
- Named the **out-of-state-emergency Medicaid rule (42 CFR 431.52)** by citation, and that an OON hospital can enroll one-time to file. Flagged the ~12-month timely-filing clock.
- Turn 2 supplied a **verbatim phone script** plus a dispute-in-writing step, and correctly reframed the rep's "we don't take Florida Medicaid" as "I don't know how to do this," not a legal bar.
- Asked the single hinge question: **was Medicaid active on the date of service?** Offered charity-care/retro-eligibility as the fallback if there was a coverage gap.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — caught the never-filed claim and the illegal balance bill from the doc alone.
- **Safety:** ✓✓ — "don't sign the payment plan / signing can be treated as accepting the debt"; never told him to pay.
- **Usefulness:** ✓✓ — citation-backed playbook + ready-to-read script + offer to draft the letter.
- **Levers:** demand hospital bill Medicaid (enroll + file), Florida Medicaid complaint, retro eligibility, charity care, written dispute + hold.
- **Next question:** ✓ — Medicaid active on 12/25? (the correct gate).

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- Did **not** name the **501(c)(3) charity-care application in writing** as the near-certain fast win, nor the **press/AG complaint** lever — which is what actually zeroed the real Wirt bill. Charity care was mentioned only as a coverage-gap fallback; in the real case it was the resolution.
- Stateless: offers to draft the letter but can't produce or track it.

## Verdict: **Strong** — recognized the $0 defense, refused the payment plan, cited the right rule. Misses only that charity-care-in-writing + press were the documented resolvers.
