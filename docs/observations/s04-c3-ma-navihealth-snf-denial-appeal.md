**Transcript:** [_raw/s04-c3-ma-navihealth-snf-denial-appeal.md](_raw/s04-c3-ma-navihealth-snf-denial-appeal.md)

## What happened
- Led with the reassurance that actually matters: **"No, you don't have to bring him home,"** then drove the **expedited QIO fast appeal** as the single most important action, with the correct deadline mechanic (**call by noon the day before** the 03/26 end date → by noon 03/25).
- Correctly explained the **financial protection**: during QIO review he stays and isn't charged; if he loses, costs generally start only after the decision date — so the **$70,000 does not apply while the appeal is pending.** This is the key fear-defusing fact and it's right.
- Identified that the denial came from **naviHealth** and framed "goals substantially met" as contradicted by the treating providers. Turn 2 correctly made the **neurologist's written medical-necessity statement** the decisive exhibit and routed it to the QIO + SNF case manager.
- Named the ~72-hour QIO decision window and the 100-day SNF benefit ceiling (both correct).
