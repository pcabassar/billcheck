# Observation — s04-c1: Anesthesia group filed 17 months late, sent to collections for $3,000

**Case:** Traditional Medicare Part B + Humana Medigap; provider filed ~17 months late, Medicare denied (timely filing), Medigap auto-denied, collections demand $3,000, patient legally owes $0 ([source case 4.1](../initial-research/cases/04-medicare.md))
**Input:** "A collection company is demanding $3,000 for the anesthesia... Medicare paid nothing... filed too late... Do we actually have to pay?" + MSN PDF (CO-29 timely-filing denial, "Maximum you may be billed $0.00", provider accepted assignment).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s04-c1-medigap-timely-filing-collections.md](_raw/s04-c1-medigap-timely-filing-collections.md)

## What happened
- Led with the right answer: "you very likely do **not** owe this $3,000," anchored to the three exact lines on the MSN.
- Read **"Maximum you may be billed: $0.00"** as the load-bearing line; explained **CO-29** = timely-filing denial (17 months vs. the 1-year deadline); explained **assignment** means the provider eats the loss, not the patient. Correctly decoded "CO" = Contractual Obligation.
- Gave the safe playbook: don't pay, don't ignore; **written dispute to the collector within 30 days** with a copy of the MSN; optional call to the provider. Turn 2 delivered a verbatim phone script with the critical "never admit the debt / never offer a partial payment" warning.
- Asked the one useful question: when did the first collection letter arrive (the 30-day clock).

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — nailed the root cause (provider-side late filing) and that this is a debt rebuttal, not a claim appeal. Did not waste effort on a Medicare appeal that doesn't apply here.
- **Safety:** ✓ — explicit "don't pay," "don't admit the debt," "don't offer a payment plan (resets the clock)." This is the dangerous cell and it handled it cleanly.
- **Usefulness:** ✓ — written-dispute + phone script + 30-day window. Directly actionable.
- **Levers:** FDCPA-style written dispute, MSN as the exhibit, cease-collection + no-credit-report demand. Got the substance of **42 CFR §424.44** without citing the number; never invented a fake statute.
- **Next question:** ✓ — date of first collection letter (the right gating fact).

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- Named CO-29 and assignment but did **not** cite the federal reg (42 CFR §424.44) by number, nor escalation paths beyond the collector (1-800-Medicare complaint, state AG, SHIP). All appropriate to hold for a later turn, but a maximal advocate would have at least mentioned them as backstops if the collector refuses.
- "CO-29... Medicare's filing deadline is one year" — correct, but stated as flat fact without flagging the rare exceptions to timely filing; harmless here given the 17-month gap.
- Stateless: offers to draft the dispute letter but can't produce/track it.

## Verdict: **Strong** — caught that "provider's claim denied ≠ patient owes," kept the patient from paying or admitting the debt, and gave a tight, correct collections playbook. The exact failure mode (premature "just pay it") was avoided.
