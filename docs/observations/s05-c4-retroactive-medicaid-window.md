# Observation — s05-c4: Uninsured 86-day stay, $1M+ billed; retroactive Medicaid window is the whole game

**Case:** [05-medicaid-dual-cob.md](../initial-research/cases/05-medicaid-dual-cob.md) §5.4 — uninsured at admission; retroactive Medicaid (covers up to 3 months pre-application) can erase the bill, but the window is the lever; H.R. 1 shrinks it starting 2027.
**Input:** "My dad was uninsured, hospitalized ~3 months, bills over a million. Someone said Medicaid might help even though he didn't have it then?" + account summary PDF ($1,042,300, $0 paid, "self-pay/uninsured", "Medicaid application started by hospital on day 65, still pending", FL resident).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s05-c4-retroactive-medicaid-window.md](_raw/s05-c4-retroactive-medicaid-window.md)

## What happened
- Led with reassurance + the core mechanism: **retroactive Medicaid pays up to 3 months before the application date**, so the pending app can cover the whole stay — "not too late."
- Read the doc precisely: app filed **day 65, still pending** → stay already inside the protected window.
- Flagged the **Florida-specific catch** unprompted: **FL did not expand Medicaid**, so a non-disabled, under-65 adult may struggle to qualify on income alone — and asked the three gating questions (age / disability / income+assets) that decide which door opens.
- Turn 2 (the timing test) was sharp: correctly explained the **retro window runs from the application date, not today**, so the stay is safe — then redirected to the **real clock: a stalled pending application denied for non-response to a request-for-information.** Gave a precise financial-counseling call script (status, RFI notices, caseworker/case number) and the subtle **"where are FL's notices being mailed?"** point.
- Charity care correctly positioned as the fallback if Medicaid is denied.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓✓ — retro-Medicaid mechanism, day-65 filing, FL non-expansion eligibility risk all caught from the doc.
- **Safety:** ✓ — "don't pay anything yet / don't agree to a payment plan"; $1M reframed as a charge number.
- **Usefulness:** ✓✓ — "start the clock and don't let it lapse" is exactly the case's job-to-be-done; the RFI-denial warning is the non-obvious, high-value insight.
- **Levers:** push the pending app to completion, demand hospital financial-counselor help, re-bill providers once approved, fair-hearing appeal on denial, charity-care fallback.
- **Next question:** ✓ — age / disability / income (T1); precise status-check call (T2).

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- Did not surface the **fair-hearing appeal (≥90 days)** on a denial, nor the **H.R. 1 / 2027 shrinking-window** legal trajectory — fine for this patient's stay, but the versioned-knowledge angle is a documented case signal.
- Stateless: can't actually track the pending-application clock it correctly identifies as the risk.

## Verdict: **Strong** — nailed the timing-is-the-lever framing and the FL non-expansion nuance; the stalled-application warning is exactly the advocate move the case calls for.
