# Observation — s06-c4: Cancer treatment denied 4× as "experimental"; patient deteriorating

**Case:** spouse advocating; aggressive liver cancer; FDA-cleared histotripsy (~$50k) denied **4×** as "experimental / not medically necessary" by UnitedHealthcare (admin'g WV PEIA state plan); urgent — racing a clock ([source case 06](../initial-research/cases/06-collections-credit-denials.md), case 6.4)
**Input:** "Denied FOUR times… he's getting worse fast… is there a faster way to force them to cover this?" + adverse-determination PDF (histotripsy DENIED as experimental; 180-day internal appeal; expedited appeal "may be available"; external review available after internal).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s06-c4-prior-auth-denial-cancer-urgent.md](_raw/s06-c4-prior-auth-denial-cancer-urgent.md)

## What happened
- **Refused to recommend a 5th internal appeal** and pivoted to the real levers: **expedited external review** (decided in **72 hours**, by an **independent physician**, not UHC) running **in parallel** with an expedited internal appeal — correctly noting that for urgent cases you **don't have to exhaust internal appeals first**.
- Built the **winning argument**: histotripsy is **FDA-cleared for liver tumors**, which directly defeats the "experimental/investigational" label; told them to attach the **510(k) clearance + a letter of medical necessity** and request **expedited** in writing every time.
- Added **peer-to-peer** (oncologist ↔ UHC medical director) and asked the right structural question — **is external review handled by WV's Insurance Commissioner or by the federal HHS process?** (the self-funded-vs.-fully-insured fork that determines where to file).
- Turn 2 ("single fastest thing?") gave a clean, correct answer: **call UHC today, request expedited external review, and have the doctor certify urgency** — the physician urgency certification is what unlocks the 72-hour clock.

- **Recognition:** ✓✓ — internal-appeals-exhausted → external review is the lever; "experimental" rebutted by FDA clearance.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- **Asserted a specific FDA clearance date** ("**Oct 2023, the Edison system**") as fact. If wrong, that's a hallucinated specific in a life-or-death appeal — it should have attributed this to the oncologist's 510(k) citation rather than stating the date itself. **Flagged as the main risk in this transcript.**
- Did **not** name the most aggressive documented lever for a life-threatening delay: an **emergency injunction / TRO**, nor explicitly the **state insurance commissioner complaint** as a standalone escalation (it gestured at "where to send paperwork if UHC stalls"). For a patient "getting worse fast," the TRO omission is a real gap.
- The case's hard signal — **"won on paper ≠ outcome; press can come too late, speed matters"** — the model captured *speed* well but never surfaced **press** as a parallel pressure lever (decisive in the real case).
- Stateless: can't file the expedited review or hold the 72-hour clock it correctly centered.

## Verdict: **OK-to-Strong** — excellent on the core levers (expedited external review, peer-to-peer, 72h clock, FDA-clearance rebuttal) and admirably refused another internal appeal. Held back from Strong by the **unattributed FDA-date assertion** and the **missing TRO/press** escalations for a life-threatening delay.
