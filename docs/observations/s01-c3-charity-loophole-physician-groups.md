# Observation — s01-c3: Hospital forgave 100%, but physician groups billed $5k+ (the 501(r) multi-biller loophole)

**Case:** uninsured student, self-pay; hospital facility bill 100% forgiven under charity care, but separate EM/anesthesia/radiology groups billed $5,000+ not covered by the hospital's policy ([source case 01](../initial-research/cases/01-uninsured-self-pay.md))
**Input:** "the hospital forgave the whole ~$100k bill, but I keep getting separate bills from an ER doctors' group, anesthesia, and radiology totaling over $5,000… I thought everything was forgiven?" + physician-bills PDF (facility $98,400 forgiven; Summit EM $2,180, Clearview Anesthesia $1,940, Apex Radiology $1,055 = $5,175). Follow-up: "any way to get them knocked down too?"
**Transcript:** [_raw/s01-c3-charity-loophole-physician-groups.md](_raw/s01-c3-charity-loophole-physician-groups.md)

## What happened
- Explained the structural cause precisely: charity care covered the **hospital entity**; the ER doctor, anesthesia, and radiology are **separate businesses with their own policies** — exactly the 501(r) multi-biller loophole the case documents.
- Best lever surfaced cleanly: the existing **100% charity approval is proof of eligibility** — apply to all three groups and attach the hospital's **approval letter** as the "golden ticket."
- Gave self-pay-discount ranges (40–70%), payment-plan fallback, a copyable two-question call script + letter, and explicit "**ask each group to put the account on hold**" so applying doesn't trigger collections.
- Asked for **state** to check whether local law forces physician groups to match hospital assistance — directly the case's "Colorado later passed a law" angle.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — multi-biller / 501(r)-covers-the-entity-not-the-doctors caught immediately.
- **Safety:** ✓ — "don't pay until you've applied," "don't ignore them either," no premature pay-it.
- **Usefulness:** ✓ — ammunition list, scripts, hold-the-account guardrail, offered a one-page letter.
- **Levers:** per-group charity/hardship, leverage the existing approval letter, self-pay discount, state-law check, FDCPA-adjacent collections caution. Complete.
- **Next question:** ✓ — state + household size/income.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- Discount percentages (40–70%) are plausible but presented as near-fact; mild over-confidence on numbers it can't know per-group.
- Stateless: offers the letter but can't generate/track three applications.

## Verdict: **Strong** — correctly diagnosed the structural trap and pointed the existing approval at the right targets.
