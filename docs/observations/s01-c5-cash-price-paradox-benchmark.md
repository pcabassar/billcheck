# Observation — s01-c5: Cash-price paradox — estimator said ~$1,400, real self-pay ~$8.4k, chargemaster $17,979

**Case:** insured (Cigna HDHP) but effectively paying cash; hospital price tool quoted ~$1,400, negotiated price $8,424, patient share $5,169, chargemaster $17,979, competitor cash ~$2,100 — documents the self-pay/chargemaster price mechanism ([source case 01](../initial-research/cases/01-uninsured-self-pay.md))
**Input:** "your online estimator said ~$1,400, the bill is way more — negotiated $8,424, my share $5,169, list price almost $18,000; another clinic quotes ~$2,100 cash. Can I push back?" + biopsy-statement PDF. Follow-up: "best way to get this down — what do I say and to who?"
**Transcript:** [_raw/s01-c5-cash-price-paradox-benchmark.md](_raw/s01-c5-cash-price-paradox-benchmark.md)

## What happened
- Correctly framed the levers: **chargemaster $17,979 is noise**; the real number is the $8,424 allowed amount; the **~6x estimator miss is the heart of the case** and the $2,100 competitor quote is the market anchor.
- Made evidence the gate: asked first for **proof of the $1,400 estimate** (screenshot/URL/confirmation) — the thing that turns "I feel overcharged" into "your own tool told me $1,400."
- Sequenced well: call Cigna to verify coding/in-network/facility-fee first (catches an inflated allowed amount), then hospital billing with a verbatim escalation script, then pivot to self-pay/prompt-pay/charity/payment-plan asks; get everything in writing, don't pay meanwhile.
- Handled the NSA edge honestly: GFE/PPDR's $400 rule **may not apply because she's insured**, but raised price-transparency obligations instead rather than over-promising.

- **Recognition:** ✓ — chargemaster-vs-allowed-vs-estimator distinction nailed; estimator gap centered.
- **Safety:** ✓ — "don't pay the $5,169 meanwhile," no premature pay-it, didn't misapply NSA.
