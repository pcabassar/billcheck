## What happened
- Named the strongest lever: **unscreened financial-assistance (FAP) eligibility** → nonprofit hospitals aren't supposed to pursue **extraordinary collection actions** (suit/garnishment) against eligible patients; FAPs often allow **retroactive application even after judgment**, which can attack the underlying balance.
- Hit the **garnishment cap** (25% of disposable earnings / 30× federal minimum-wage floor) and flagged that **$214/period on near-minimum wage may exceed the legal limit** and can be challenged.
- Gave a concrete **stop-the-bleeding** route — a **slow-pay / claim-for-exemption / installment motion** in Shelby County to halt the garnishment and set affordable payments — sequenced ahead of the slower FAP attack.
- Pointed to **Memphis Area Legal Services** + **TN Justice Center**, and (turn 2) surfaced the **press history** ("Methodist Le Bonheur specifically got national scrutiny… for suing its own employees") and **anti-retaliation** for the employer angle. Asked the dependents question (changes exemption math).
