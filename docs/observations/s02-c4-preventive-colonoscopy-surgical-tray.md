# Observation — s02-c4: Surgical-tray add-on charged on a $0 preventive colonoscopy

**Case:** commercial ACA-marketplace BCBS-IL, in-network GI group; preventive colonoscopy at $0 but a $250 "surgical tray" (HCPCS A4550) applied to deductible; the documented real case was won via appeal + DOI + press ([source case 2.4](../initial-research/cases/02-commercial-in-network.md))
**Input:** "Routine screening colonoscopy, supposed to be 100% free… colonoscopy was $0 but there's a $250 'surgical tray' on my deductible… can they tack that on?" + GI statement PDF (CPT 45378-33 $0 patient; A4550 surgical tray $250 to deductible; patient resp. $250).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s02-c4-preventive-colonoscopy-surgical-tray.md](_raw/s02-c4-preventive-colonoscopy-surgical-tray.md)

## What happened
- Confirmed the instinct and cited the rule cleanly: under ACA the preventive screening (noted the **-33 modifier**) must be 100% covered, and federal guidance treats supplies/facility/anesthesia as **integral** — they can't be carved out and billed separately.
- Diagnosed the likely mechanism: the A4550 tray line wasn't tied to the preventive code, so it slipped to the deductible — a coding error, not a real charge.
- Gave a verbatim insurer call script ("billed with modifier 33… please reprocess the A4550 tray as part of the preventive service") and the smart move of asking the insurer to **cite the specific language** that permits separate supply billing (they won't have it).
- Correctly raised the **polyp-removal exception** and resolved it accurately: even a screening that turns diagnostic via polyp removal stays preventive under federal guidance, so the tray shouldn't land on the deductible either way.
- Follow-up cleanly distinguished the **provider statement (uploaded) vs. the BCBS EOB** and asked for the EOB as the document that pinpoints where the breakdown happened.
