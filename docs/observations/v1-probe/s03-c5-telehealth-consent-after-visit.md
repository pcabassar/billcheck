You have a strong case here, and the document spells it out clearly. Let me explain why, then propose how we fight it.

**Why you're on solid ground:**

The federal **No Surprises Act** protects you in exactly this situation. You used an **in-network facility/system** (Mount Sinai), but got an **out-of-network rendering physician**. For an OON provider at an in-network facility to bill you the difference (balance billing), they must get your **valid written consent at least 72 hours before** the visit. Your consent form was signed at **4:15 PM — after the visit ended at 2:42 PM the same day.** That consent is invalid on its face, and it sounds like they've already admitted as much.

When valid consent isn't obtained, the protections kick in: you can only be billed your **in-network cost-sharing amount** — which lines up with the ~$60 the app showed you, not $660.

**What this means:** You should owe roughly your in-network share (~$60), and the $660 balance bill is improper.
