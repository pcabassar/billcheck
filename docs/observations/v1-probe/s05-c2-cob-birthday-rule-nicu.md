First, take a breath — that $270,951 balance is almost certainly **not** what you'll actually owe. This is a coordination-of-benefits (COB) problem between two insurers, and it's very fixable. Let me explain what's happening and what we do.

## What's going on

**The "birthday rule"** decides which of two parents' plans pays first for a child. It's not about whose birthday is older — it's about whose birthday falls **earlier in the calendar year** (month and day, ignoring the year). Whichever parent's birthday comes first in the year, that parent's plan is **primary**.

So Blue KC is claiming your husband's plan (CommunityCare OK) should be primary because his birthday lands earlier in the year. The key thing:

- **A reversal does NOT mean you owe the full bill.** It means the wrong insurer went first. The fix is for the *correct* primary (husband's CommunityCare) to process the claim, then Blue KC reprocesses as secondary. Between the two plans, the vast majority of that $270K gets paid.
- Right now there's **no claim on file with CommunityCare** — that's the actual gap. Nothing has been submitted to the plan that's supposed to pay first.

## One thing I want to flag

Your Blue KC plan is **self-funded** (employer-administered). Self-funded plans aren't bound by state birthday-rule laws the same way — they follow their own plan document. So I want to verify Blue KC even applied the rule correctly before we assume your husband's plan is truly primary. We may need to confirm both birthdays and check the plan language.

2. **Have you gotten a written EOB or denial letter from Blue KC** showing the reversal, or just the hospital's summary?
