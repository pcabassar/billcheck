### billcheck
Great news on the $98,400 — that's a huge win. Let me explain what's happening with these other bills, because it's a really common surprise.

**Why you're still getting billed**

When you go to the ER, you often get treated by doctors who *don't actually work for the hospital* — they're separate companies that bill independently. That's exactly what these three are:

- **Summit Emergency Physicians** — the ER doctor who treated you
- **Clearview Anesthesia Associates** — anesthesia
- **Apex Radiology Group** — read your scans/X-rays

The hospital's charity care program covered the **facility's** bill (the room, nursing, supplies). It usually does **not** automatically cover these outside groups, because the hospital can only forgive its own charges. So this isn't a mistake — but it's also **not the end of the road.**

**Do you have to pay these? Often, no — or much less.**

Here's the key: if you qualified for the hospital's charity care, you very likely qualify for financial assistance with these groups too. Each of these companies typically has its own charity care / financial assistance policy, and many of them will reduce or fully forgive bills for patients in your situation. You'd apply to each one separately.

So the plan is usually:
1. Apply to each group's financial assistance program (citing that the hospital already granted you full charity care — that's strong evidence).
2. For any that won't fully forgive, ask about uninsured/self-pay discounts and the fair price.
