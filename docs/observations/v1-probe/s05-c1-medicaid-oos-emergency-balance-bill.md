**The good news.** Federal law requires state Medicaid programs to cover **emergency services for their enrollees even when the care happens in another state**, including at hospitals that aren't normally in the Medicaid network. A heart attack (acute MI) is the textbook definition of an emergency. So Florida Medicaid can be required to pay for an out-of-state ER admission like yours.

### billcheck
No — that's not the whole truth, and it's the part of the conversation where a lot of patients give up and start paying. Here's the real picture:

**"We don't take Florida Medicaid" ≠ "you owe us $77,000."**

A hospital not being *enrolled* with Florida Medicaid means they're not already set up to bill them. It does **not** mean they can't get paid by Medicaid, and it does **not** make you personally responsible. For an out-of-state emergency, the hospital generally has options:

- **Enroll as an out-of-state Medicaid provider** (Florida allows this, often retroactively, specifically for emergencies like yours), then bill the claim, or
- Work with Florida Medicaid's process for **out-of-state emergency claims**.

The front-line billing rep usually doesn't know or won't volunteer this — they just see "not enrolled" and push the payment plan. You have to ask for it specifically and put it in writing.

**The other key fact:** You can't be balance-billed for the difference. If Florida Medicaid is responsible for your emergency care, the hospital's recourse is to get Medicaid to pay — not to collect $77K from you.

**What to say to them — your script:**

> "This was an emergency admission for a heart attack on December 25th. I was enrolled in Florida Medicaid at the time, and federal law requires Medicaid to cover out-of-state emergency care. I'm not declining to cooperate — but I am not able to be balance-billed for this. Please enroll as an out-of-state Medicaid provider so you can submit this claim to Florida Medicaid, or tell me in writing why you can't. I'm not authorizing a payment plan, and I'm asking you to hold collections while this is submitted to Medicaid."
