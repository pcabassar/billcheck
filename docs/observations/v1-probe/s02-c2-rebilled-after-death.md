**You do not have to just pay this because they sent it.** A bill arriving 14 months later, after the account was settled and paid, deserves real scrutiny before a dollar changes hands. Here's what stands out:

1. **The statement doesn't explain *why* the balance changed.** It just says "ACCOUNT ADJUSTED" and shows your responsibility jumped from $823.15 to $1,916.31. A more-than-double increase needs a reason. Did the insurer reprocess the claim? Was there a coding correction? You're entitled to know.

2. **The insurer side may not match.** This is the key issue. The hospital is allowed to bill you only what your insurance plan determines is your responsibility — your deductible, copay, and coinsurance per the *Explanation of Benefits (EOB)* from Blue Cross. If the hospital's "revised" number doesn't match a corresponding revised EOB from BCBS, they may be billing you for an amount they're not allowed to charge.

3. **Timing matters.** Many insurer contracts and state rules limit how long a provider has to rebill, and Illinois has rules here. This may be past a window.

**The single most useful thing right now:** Do you have an EOB from Blue Cross for this stay — either the original one from 2025, or a new/revised one that arrived recently? That document is what actually tells us what you legitimately owe. The hospital bill alone doesn't.

If you can find it (or tell me you can't locate one), that decides our next move. In the meantime, would it help if I drafted a written request to the hospital demanding an itemized statement and a written explanation of why the balance was adjusted?
