### billcheck
That QMB detail changes **everything**. This is the most important thing you've told me.

**QMB = Qualified Medicare Beneficiary.** If you're in the QMB program, federal law says **you cannot be billed for Medicare cost-sharing** — no deductibles, no coinsurance, no copays. Providers must bill Medicare (and then Medicaid), and they have to write off whatever isn't paid. **Billing a QMB patient is illegal** under federal law (the Social Security Act). This is called "improper balance billing," and CMS takes it seriously.

Ambulance rides are exactly where this goes wrong — the ambulance company often skips billing Medicare/Medicaid and dumps the bill straight on the patient or into collections. That looks like what may have happened here.

**So to your original question — do you owe this?** Based on what you're describing, very likely **not a dime**, assuming the ride was a Medicare-covered service and you were QMB-enrolled on the date of service (Jan 15, 2026).
