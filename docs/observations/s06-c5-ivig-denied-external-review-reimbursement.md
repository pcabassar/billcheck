# Observation — s06-c5: IVIG denied 2 years, $90k out of pocket; external review won, wants money back + coverage secured

**Case:** small-fiber neuropathy; Anthem (commercial → COBRA, VA) denied IVIG as "not medically necessary" 2 yrs; ~$90k paid out of pocket; **external review overturned** the denial **prospectively only**; $90k unaddressed; COBRA expiring ([source case 06](../initial-research/cases/06-collections-credit-denials.md), case 6.5)
**Input:** "I finally won… ruling only covers me going forward… I paid about $90,000 out of pocket… COBRA running out — can I get the $90k back, and how do I not end up back here?" + VA Bureau of Insurance external-review decision PDF (denial OVERTURNED, prospective scope, ~$90k NOT addressed).

## What happened
- Reframed the win into the **retroactive-reimbursement argument**: the reviewer's "**not medically necessary** denial was **wrong**" is a clinical finding that **applies to the already-paid infusions** — "prospective only" is the *scope of the request*, not a finding the past care was different. Strong, correct framing.
- Surfaced the three things that actually decide recovery: **ERISA self-funded vs. fully insured** (different forum/leverage), **whether past denials were formally appealed vs. just paid cash**, and — critically — **timely-filing deadlines** ("two years is at the edge… every week matters"). That's the clock for this case and the model flagged it as urgent.
- Gave the **coverage-transition** plan COBRA demands: lock a **letter of medical necessity into the medical record**, **check the next plan's formulary/prior-auth before enrolling**, treat the external-review decision as **portable evidence**, and **don't miss the COBRA-ending Special Enrollment Period** (with the ACA-may-be-cheaper note).
- Turn 2 gave an **honest outcome range** (best/middle/worst), pointed to the **VA Bureau of Insurance** + an **ERISA/benefits attorney on contingency** for a $90k stake, and offered to draft the demand letter.
