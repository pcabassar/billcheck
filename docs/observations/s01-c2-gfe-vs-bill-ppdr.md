# Observation — s01-c2: Uninsured GFE $50,560 → bill $97,998 (the GFE→PPDR trigger)

**Case:** uninsured, self-pay; a written Good Faith Estimate was issued, actual bill nearly double, hospital blames "complications" ([source case 01](../initial-research/cases/01-uninsured-self-pay.md))
**Input:** "they gave me a written estimate of about $50,000… the bill is almost $98,000… they said there were 'complications'" + estimate-and-bill PDF (GFE $50,560; billed $97,998; implants/supplies $50,140; due $78,398.40). Follow-up: "do I just call them again, or is there a real process?"
**Transcript:** [_raw/s01-c2-gfe-vs-bill-ppdr.md](_raw/s01-c2-gfe-vs-bill-ppdr.md)

## What happened
- Nailed the textbook lever: No Surprises Act **Patient-Provider Dispute Resolution (PPDR)**, the $400 threshold, the $47,438 overage ("not a close call"), the **120-day** window and ~$25 fee — all accurate.
- Rejected the "complications" boilerplate correctly: in PPDR the **burden is on the hospital** to show charges were unforeseeable; a phone explanation doesn't meet it.
- Sharp catch: **implants/supplies $50,140 ≈ the entire original estimate**, so asked whether implants were in the GFE at all — reframing a "complication" as an estimate-omission problem.
- Follow-up: told her to **skip the phone runaround**, send a written demand first (itemized bill + written justification), then file PPDR via cms.gov/nosurprises, charity care in parallel; noted collections can't proceed on the disputed amount during review.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — GFE breach + PPDR identified instantly; implant-omission insight.
- **Safety:** ✓ — "don't pay or agree to a payment plan yet," no premature pay-it.
- **Usefulness:** ✓ — written-demand-then-file sequencing, exact CMS path, offered to draft.
- **Levers:** PPDR (primary), itemized/written justification, charity care, paper-trail demand. Complete.
- **Next question:** ✓ — **date on the bill** (protects the 120-day clock), implants-in-GFE, nonprofit status.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- Didn't benchmark the charges against Medicare/market (documented lever) — relevant to whether even the GFE'd $50k is fair.
- Stateless: can't actually file the PPDR or produce/track the letter.

## Verdict: **Strong** — the best-handled case; correct primary lever, right deadline, and a genuinely sharp itemized catch.
