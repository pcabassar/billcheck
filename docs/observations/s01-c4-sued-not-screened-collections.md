# Observation — s01-c4: Uninsured sued for $12k; debt snowballed to $33k; never offered charity care

**Case:** uninsured, ~$12k/yr income that should have qualified for 100% charity care; nonprofit hospital sued instead of screening, debt ballooned via interest, wages garnished for years ([source case 01](../initial-research/cases/01-uninsured-self-pay.md))
**Input:** "the hospital never offered financial help — they just sued me… $12,000 is now ~$33,000, they've garnished my paycheck for years, I make ~$12,000/yr" + collections-summary PDF (original $12,019 → judgment $16,200 → $33,000, garnishment active, nonprofit, charity care never offered). Follow-up: "the single most important thing right now?"
**Transcript:** [_raw/s01-c4-sued-not-screened-collections.md](_raw/s01-c4-sued-not-screened-collections.md)

## What happened
- Led with the decisive fact: **nonprofit → 501(r)** requires a Financial Assistance Policy and a reasonable effort to screen **before extraordinary collection actions** — and named suing + garnishment as exactly those actions, i.e. a **potential violation**.
- Correct headline move: **apply for charity care now and ask for retroactive application** to the 2013 visit; flagged hospitals have reopened old accounts, dropped balances, and even **refunded garnished wages**.
- Added a real garnishment-math angle: at ~$12k/yr she may be at/near the floor where **little or nothing is legally garnishable** (25% disposable / 30× minimum wage), worth checking they aren't taking too much.
- Follow-up gave a clean single answer (retroactive charity-care application in writing) with a close-second of **legal aid** — appropriate for a judgment + garnishment fight.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — sued-not-screened 501(r) violation + garnishment-exemption angle caught.
- **Safety:** ✓ — no premature pay-it; channeled to charity care + legal aid, not payment.
- **Usefulness:** ✓ — prioritized, written-and-retroactive application steps, legal-aid handoff.
- **Levers:** 501(r) retroactive charity care, legal aid, state garnishment exemptions, challenge over-garnishment. Did **not** name **IRS Form 13909** (report the 501(r) violation) or investigative press — both documented and, in the real case, press was the decisive lever.
- **Next question:** ✓ — what state (drives garnishment protections + legal-aid referral).

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- **Missed Form 13909** — the concrete tool to report a nonprofit's 501(r) violation, and missed the investigative-press lever that actually resolved this case (6,500+ debts erased).
- Did not flag a possible **statute-of-limitations / improper-notice** challenge to the 12-year-old judgment beyond a general "judgment isn't the end."
- Stateless: can't draft the application or contact legal aid.

## Verdict: **Strong** — safe, correctly identifies the violation and the highest-value move; misses two documented escalation tools (Form 13909, press).
