# Observation — seed-03: Sold as a matching call, billed as a clinical session ($179)

**Case:** insured; a "matching/onboarding" call billed as CPT 90834 to the deductible, no therapist delivered; provider claims "legally unable to refund" ([source case 03 — Pedro's first-party case](../initial-research/cases/index.md))
**Input:** "Sold as a matching call, nobody said it'd cost anything… now a $179 'clinical session' charge… they refuse to refund, saying 'legally unable to'" + claim-detail PDF.
**Transcript:** [_raw/seed-03-two-chairs-misrepresentation.md](_raw/seed-03-two-chairs-misrepresentation.md)

## What happened
- Identified the **CPT 90834 miscoding** angle (a matching/intake call ≠ a 45-min psychotherapy session) as the cleanest lever.
- **Cracked the "legally unable to refund" wall:** explained its grain of truth (can't refund while the claim stands), then exposed it as a dodge — the fix is to **retract/correct the claim**, not refund.
- Reframed the ask from *"refund me"* → *"retract the 90834 claim billed 05/12"*, told the user to get into **writing**, set a **deadline + named escalation** (insurer / state board / AG), and open an **insurer dispute in parallel**.
- Asked the unlocking question (**licensed therapist or coordinator?**) and offered to **draft the message**.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓✓ — miscoding + misrepresentation, on a non-arithmetic case.
- **Safety:** ✓ — no bad advice; correctly narrow on what "legally unable" means.
- **Usefulness:** ✓✓ — reconstructed the real case's advocacy playbook nearly verbatim.
- **Levers:** recode/retract claim, insurer dispute, consent/surprise-billing angle, deadline + regulator escalation.
- **Next question:** ✓ — who ran the call.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- This is the **persistent-campaign** case — the prototype gives the playbook but **can't run the weeks-long, multi-channel campaign** (escalation ladder, deadlines, auto follow-up, paper-trail capture). The single biggest architectural gap.
- Didn't name **chargeback / BBB / state DFPI** specifically (named insurer + board/AG); chargeback may not apply since it's billed to insurance, not a card — defensible.
- Offers to draft the email but has no artifact tool to actually produce/version it.

## Verdict: **Strong** — the hardest case (pure advocacy), handled with real sophistication. The gap is *execution & persistence*, not *reasoning*.
