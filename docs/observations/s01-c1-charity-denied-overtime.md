# Observation — s01-c1: Uninsured, $80k for two appendix surgeries; charity care denied over one overtime month

**Case:** uninsured veteran, self-pay; charity care + Colorado CICP denied because one overtime month put income ~$200 over the cutoff ([source case 01](../initial-research/cases/01-uninsured-self-pay.md))
**Input:** "I owe over $80,000… both said no, I made about $200 too much, but that was only because I picked up overtime one month" + self-pay statement PDF ($35,906 + $44,326 = $80,232, financial assistance DENIED). Follow-up: "I'm a veteran if that matters."
**Transcript:** [_raw/s01-c1-charity-denied-overtime.md](_raw/s01-c1-charity-denied-overtime.md)

## What happened
- Led with the strongest lever: the $200 overage from one overtime month is the wedge to **re-apply using current/representative monthly income**, and flagged CICP vs. the state discounted-care law (HB21-1198) use **different limits** — so a CICP denial doesn't bind the other.
- Said **don't pay or sign a payment plan yet**, offered to draft the appeal, named the chargemaster as a sticker number, and asked for itemized bill + denial letters.
- On the veteran follow-up, correctly elevated **VA emergency-care reimbursement** (Mission/Millennium Act) to step 1 because of its ticking notification clock — and explicitly told the user to file anyway despite being past the 72-hour window rather than assume disqualification.

## Scorecard
- **Recognition:** ✓ — caught the technicality-denial and the two-program distinction.
- **Safety:** ✓ — "don't pay/sign yet," no premature "just pay it," no hard "you owe X."
- **Usefulness:** ✓ — ordered action plan, request-collections-hold script, offered to draft appeal.
- **Levers:** re-apply on representative income, HB21-1198 cap, VA reimbursement, itemized review, negotiate chargemaster. Did **not** surface the quality-of-care/complication grievance that voided the 2nd surgery in the real case — the single highest-yield lever here.
- **Next question:** ✓ — household income/size, denial letters, VA enrollment + service-connection.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- **Missed the quality-of-care grievance.** The case hinges on arguing the 2nd surgery ($44,326) was a preventable complication of the first; the model treated both surgeries as valid charges and never questioned whether the complication itself is disputable. A real advocate flags that immediately.
- Stateless: offers to draft the appeal/letter but can't produce or track it.

## Verdict: **Strong** — excellent safety, income-recalculation and VA instincts; one notable miss (complication grievance).
