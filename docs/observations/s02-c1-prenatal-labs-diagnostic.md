**Case:** commercial Anthem BCBS-TX, in-network hospital lab; four prenatal screens (Rh, HepB, HepC, syphilis) billed diagnostic; internal appeal denied; $2,390 owed ([source case 2.1](../initial-research/cases/02-commercial-in-network.md))
**Input:** "I'm pregnant… routine prenatal blood work… told preventive was free… now I owe $2,390 and my appeal got denied, said 'diagnostic'" + EOB PDF (billed $9,520 / allowed $6,700 / paid $4,310 / member resp. $2,390; appeal denied).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s02-c1-prenatal-labs-diagnostic.md](_raw/s02-c1-prenatal-labs-diagnostic.md)

## What happened
- Opened by clearing the user of blame, then taught **preventive vs. diagnostic** as billing labels, not medical facts.
- Named the exact ACA mechanism: these four are the standard early prenatal panel; the likely culprit is the **diagnosis code** (named Z34 routine-pregnancy code) vs. a suspected-condition code.
- Correctly escalated past the denied internal appeal to **external/IRO review** (named the ~4-month window, Texas IRO), gave a verbatim billing-call script and a written appeal argument.
- Said **don't pay the $2,390 yet**, asked the one diagnostic question that sizes the case (truly routine vs. symptom-driven).
