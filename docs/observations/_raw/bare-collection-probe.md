## Your most important move: the 30-day window

## QMB means you almost certainly owe $0

QMB stands for **Qualified Medicare Beneficiary**. Under federal law, if you're in the QMB program, you are **legally protected from being billed for Medicare cost-sharing** — that means deductibles, copays, and coinsurance. Providers who accept Medicare are **prohibited from billing you** for these amounts. This is called "improper balance billing," and it's against federal rules.

An ambulance ride is a Medicare-covered service. So that $250 "patient balance"? For a QMB beneficiary, that is exactly the kind of charge you're **not supposed to be billed for at all.** Medicare pays its share, Medicaid picks up the rest (or it's written off), and your responsibility is zero.

In short: **you very likely do not owe this $271.40 — not the $250, not the interest.**

## What very likely went wrong

One of these happened:
- The ambulance company never billed Medicare/Medicaid correctly, or
- They billed it but then wrongly tried to collect the leftover from you anyway, or
- It was never run through your QMB status at all.

None of these are your problem to pay for. They're the provider's billing error.

## What to do now

**1. Send the dispute letter (within your 30-day window).** Now it has teeth. Say specifically:
> "I am a Qualified Medicare Beneficiary (QMB). Under federal law, I am not liable for Medicare cost-sharing. This debt appears to be improper balance billing. I dispute this debt in full and request verification, including proof that this charge was billed to Medicare and Medicaid."

Send it certified mail, keep a copy.

**2. Do NOT set up a payment plan or pay anything.** Paying a debt you don't legally owe is the wrong move here, and there's no need.

**3. Report it if they don't back off.** Improper QMB billing can be reported to:
- **Medicare: 1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227)** — tell them you're a QMB being balance-billed.
- Your **state Medicaid office / SHIP counselor** (free Medicare help — I can help you find your state's number).
