Good — here's the order of operations and exactly what to say at each stop. Don't skip steps; each one either lowers the bill or strengthens your case for the next call.

## Step 1: Call Cigna (verify the claim is even right)

Before negotiating, make sure the $8,424 itself isn't an error. Ask:

- "Was this processed **in-network**?"
- "What **procedure (CPT) codes** were billed, and is the allowed amount correct for those codes?"
- "Was this billed with a **facility fee** on top of the procedure?"
- "Did my **deductible and any coinsurance** apply correctly?"

**Why:** A wrong code or an out-of-network slip can inflate that allowed amount. If you find an error here, that's the cleanest win — Cigna fixes it and your share drops automatically.

## Step 2: Call the hospital billing office (the main event)

Ask for a **supervisor or patient billing advocate**, not the first person who picks up. Lead with the estimator. Script:

> "Before my procedure I used your online price estimator, which quoted approximately **$1,400** for this service. I relied on that to make my decision. The bill is now **$5,169** — more than three times what your own tool told me. I'd like you to honor the estimate, or adjust the charge to a fair rate."

If they push back, escalate calmly:

> "Your published estimator is a price-transparency tool I'm entitled to rely on. A nearby clinic quotes **$2,100 cash** for the identical service. I'm asking you to bring this in line with what I was quoted and what the market charges."

**Have ready:** the estimator screenshot/quote, the $2,100 cash quote (call the other clinic and get it confirmed in writing if you can).

## Step 3: If they won't honor the estimate, pivot to these asks (in order)

1. **Match the cash-pay/self-pay rate.** Hospitals often have a self-pay price far below the negotiated amount. Ask: "What's your self-pay or prompt-pay cash price for this?" — it may be near that $2,100.
2. **Prompt-pay discount.** Many will knock off 20–40% if you pay quickly in one lump.
3. **Financial assistance / charity care.** Nonprofit hospitals are legally required to offer this. Ask: "Do you have a financial assistance policy, and can you send me the application?" Even high earners sometimes qualify on a sliding scale.
4. **Interest-free payment plan.** Your fallback if the number stays high — never put this on a credit card.

## Step 4: Get everything in writing

Whatever they agree to, ask for it **in writing/email before you pay**. And **don't pay the $5,169 in the meantime** — paying signals you accept it and weakens your leverage.

## A few rules of thumb

- **Be polite but persistent.** "I relied on your estimate" is reasonable and hard to argue with. You're not being difficult; you're holding them to their own number.
- **Take notes:** date, name of person, what they agreed to.
- If the billing rep stonewalls, ask for the **patient advocate** or the **hospital's price-transparency contact** specifically.
