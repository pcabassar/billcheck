## What the "birthday rule" actually means

When a child is covered by both parents' plans, the **birthday rule** decides which plan pays first: it's whichever parent's birthday falls **earlier in the calendar year** (just the month/day — the year/age doesn't matter).

So Blue KC reviewed this and decided your husband's plan (CommunityCare OK) should be primary because his birthday comes first in the year. That part may well be correct. **But here's the key point:** the birthday rule just changes the *order* the plans pay in. It does **not** mean you lose coverage. Between two plans, the vast majority of this should still be paid by insurance.

## What went wrong — and it's fixable

The hospital did two things:
1. Let Blue KC claw back its $35,721, AND
2. **Never billed the secondary insurer** ("CommunityCare OK: no claim on file").

So they reversed one payer and didn't bill the next. That's why the *whole* balance dumped on you. That's a process gap, not a real debt.

The correct sequence should be:
- **CommunityCare (husband's plan) pays as primary**
- **Blue KC pays as secondary** — and as the secondary plan it typically picks up much of what primary doesn't, including deductibles/coinsurance

You may end up owing a copay or deductible amount, but nothing remotely close to $271,000.

## What to do now — in order
