## This denial probably violates federal law

Your Molina plan is an **ACA marketplace plan**, which means it's required to cover **emergency services** under the Affordable Care Act's "prudent layperson" standard. Federal law says:

- **No prior authorization can be required for emergency care.** You literally cannot be denied for not getting pre-approval for an emergency — that's not allowed for ACA plans. Being airlifted unconscious is the textbook definition of an emergency.
- **Out-of-network emergency care must be covered as if it were in-network.** The fact that Phoenix Regional was out-of-network doesn't change your protection here.

So "INPATIENT STAY NOT PRIOR-AUTHORIZED" is not a legal reason to deny emergency care on a plan like yours.

## You may also have No Surprises Act protection

The federal **No Surprises Act** (effective 2022) protects you from balance billing for **emergency services** and **air ambulance** from out-of-network providers. You should only owe your normal in-network cost-sharing (deductible/copay/coinsurance) — not the full $15,312 or $59,000. Air ambulance balance billing in particular is specifically banned.
