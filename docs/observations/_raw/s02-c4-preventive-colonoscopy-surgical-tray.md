### billcheck
Your instinct is right — this charge shouldn't be there, and you have a strong basis to dispute it.

**Here's the rule that backs you up:**

Under the ACA, when a colonoscopy is billed as a preventive screening (which yours was — note the CPT 45378 with modifier **-33**, the code that flags it as preventive), your plan has to cover it at **100% with no cost-sharing**. And federal guidance is explicit that this means the *entire service*, not just the scope part. The supplies, the facility, anesthesia, and the surgical tray are all considered **integral to the screening** and can't be carved out and billed to you separately.

In plain terms: a "surgical tray" isn't a separate optional add-on — it's part of doing the colonoscopy. They can't take a service that's supposed to cost you nothing and peel off a $250 supply fee. That's exactly the kind of cost-shifting the rule prohibits.

**What's likely going on:** The colonoscopy line was coded correctly as preventive, but the A4550 tray line wasn't tied to that preventive code, so it slipped through to your deductible. This is usually a billing/coding error, not a real charge you owe.

**What to do:**
1. **Call your insurer (BCBS-IL) first.** Say: "My screening colonoscopy was preventive, billed with modifier 33. Federal preventive-care rules require all integral services, including supplies, to be covered at 100%. Please reprocess the A4550 surgical tray charge as part of the preventive service." Ask them to reprocess the claim.
2. If they push back, ask them to point to the specific plan or regulatory language that lets them bill supplies separately on a preventive screening — they won't have one.
3. You can also call Prairie GI and ask them not to send this to collections while the claim is being reprocessed.

**One thing I'd check:** Did they find and remove any polyps during the procedure? If so, the screening can sometimes shift to "diagnostic" and cost-sharing rules change a bit — but even then, the colonoscopy was still billed at $0 here, and federal guidance says a screening that becomes diagnostic *due to polyp removal* still stays preventive. So either way, the tray shouldn't land on your deductible.
