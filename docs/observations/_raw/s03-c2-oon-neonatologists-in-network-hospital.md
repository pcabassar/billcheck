### billcheck
Your instinct is right. This is very likely an illegal bill, and the fact that both sides told you "nothing we can do" doesn't make it true.

## Why this is almost certainly protected

The **federal No Surprises Act** (effective January 2022) bans exactly this situation: out-of-network providers billing you extra when you're treated at an **in-network facility** and you didn't choose them. NICU care is the textbook example — you cannot consent to or shop for a neonatologist while your newborn is in intensive care.

Under this law, for these "ancillary"/emergency-type services at an in-network facility:
- You can only be charged your **in-network cost-sharing** (your normal deductible/coinsurance/copay).
- The out-of-network provider and your insurer must settle the rest between themselves — **not bill you the difference.**
- A neonatologist legally **cannot** get a valid waiver of these protections from you. They're non-waivable for this type of care.

That $12,531 is "balance billing," and it is what the law prohibits.

## What's likely happening

Your plan is **BCBS Illinois, employer group, fully insured** — that's important. Fully insured plans are squarely covered by federal protections, and Illinois also has its own surprise-billing law. So you have two layers of protection here.

The bill shows insurance "allowed" only $2,092.97. The law says the provider should be paid a proper amount through the federal dispute process — but that fight is between Lurie and BCBS. **You should owe only your in-network share, period.**

## What to do next

1. **Stop talking to phone reps who say "nothing we can do."** Put everything in writing.
2. **File a No Surprises Act complaint** with the federal No Surprises Help Desk: **1-800-985-3059** or cms.gov/nosurprises. This is the fastest lever — it triggers a real review.
3. **Also file with the Illinois Department of Insurance** (they handle fully insured plans). They take these seriously.
4. **Send the provider a written dispute** stating the bill violates the No Surprises Act (NICU care at an in-network facility, no valid consent possible) and asking them to rebill only your in-network cost-sharing.
5. **Don't pay the $12,531** while disputing. Paying can complicate getting it back.
