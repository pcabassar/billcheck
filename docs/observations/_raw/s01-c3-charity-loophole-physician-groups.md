### billcheck
This is one of the most common and frustrating surprises in medical billing — and the good news is you likely have a real path to getting these reduced or wiped out too.

## Why this is happening

The $98,400 charity care covered the **hospital facility** charges — the building, nursing, supplies, room, etc. But the doctors who treated you often **don't work for the hospital.** They're separate businesses that bill independently:

- **Summit Emergency Physicians** — the ER doctor
- **Clearview Anesthesia Associates** — anesthesia
- **Apex Radiology Group** — read your X-rays/CT scans

So the hospital's charity program, by default, only forgave its own bill. These three groups each have their *own* policies. You aren't being double-billed or scammed — this is just how the system is fragmented.

## The key point: you very likely don't have to pay all of this

Here's the leverage you have:

1. **You already qualified for charity care.** A hospital approved you for 100% forgiveness on a $98,400 bill. That's strong proof your income/assets are low enough to qualify almost anywhere. Each physician group has its own financial assistance / charity policy — **apply to all three** and send them a copy of your hospital's charity care approval letter. Many groups will match or come close.

2. **Uninsured "self-pay" rates are inflated.** Even if a group won't fully forgive, they almost always offer a large self-pay discount (often 40–70%) just for asking, plus interest-free payment plans.

3. **Don't pay anything until you've applied.** Paying now would be the worst move — it's far harder to get money back than to negotiate before paying.

## Your next steps

- **Call each of the three groups** and ask two questions: "Do you have a financial assistance or charity care program?" and "What's your uninsured/self-pay discount?"
- **Get the hospital's charity care approval letter** if you don't already have it — that's your golden ticket for these applications.
- **Put nothing in writing as a promise to pay** until you've explored assistance.
