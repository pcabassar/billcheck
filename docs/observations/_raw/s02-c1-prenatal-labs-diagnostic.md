First — no, you didn't do anything wrong. This is a coding problem on the hospital's end, and it's a common one. Let me explain what's going on, because you have a strong case here.

## What "diagnostic" vs. "preventive" means

These are billing labels, not medical facts:

- **Preventive/screening** = routine tests done *because* you're pregnant, with no symptoms. Under the ACA, these are covered at **$0 out of pocket** on most plans.
- **Diagnostic** = a test done to investigate a specific symptom or known problem. These get run through your deductible and coinsurance — which is how you ended up with $2,390.

The four tests on your EOB — **Rh factor/antibody, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, and syphilis (RPR)** — are *exactly* the standard early prenatal panel that every pregnant patient gets. These are explicitly listed preventive services for pregnancy. The hospital coded them "diagnostic" anyway, and your insurer just rubber-stamped that label.

## Why this matters legally

Under the ACA, plans must cover certain prenatal screenings with no cost-sharing. The specific tests you got are on the recommended preventive list for pregnancy. The issue is almost certainly the **diagnosis code** the lab attached — if it used a "screening/routine pregnancy" code (Z34) these would process as $0. If it used a code suggesting they were testing for a *suspected* condition, they get billed as diagnostic.

So this is a fixable coding issue, not a "you owe this" situation.

## Your next steps

You've done one internal appeal. Don't stop — here's where the leverage is:

1. **Call the hospital lab billing department** and ask: *"What diagnosis codes were submitted on claim TX5582019? These were routine prenatal screenings with no symptoms — why weren't they coded as preventive (Z34)?"* Ask them to **rebill with the correct screening codes.** Often this alone fixes it.

2. **File a second-level / external appeal with Anthem**, citing that these are ACA-required preventive prenatal services that should be covered at $0. The "internal appeal denied" status means you may now be eligible for an **independent external review** — that's a big deal because an outside reviewer, not Anthem, decides.

3. **Don't pay the $2,390 yet.** You don't owe it while this is in dispute, and paying can make it harder to recover.
