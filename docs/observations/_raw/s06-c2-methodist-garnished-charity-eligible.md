- **They can't take unlimited amounts.** Under federal and Tennessee law, garnishment is capped at **25% of your disposable earnings**, OR the amount your weekly pay exceeds 30× federal minimum wage — whichever is *less*. **The bigger lever: an employer can't punish or retaliate against you over a garnishment, and a nonprofit hospital garnishing its own low-wage employee — who may have qualified for free care — is exactly the kind of fact pattern that gets cases dropped or settled fast once an advocate or reporter gets near it.** Methodist Le Bonheur specifically got national scrutiny a few years back for suing its own employees and low-income patients.
