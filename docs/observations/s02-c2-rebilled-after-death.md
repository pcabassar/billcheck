# Observation — s02-c2: Re-billed $1,093 a year after settling and paying the EOB

**Case:** commercial BCBS-IL, in-network hospital; EOB settled & paid ($823.15), then a revised bill 14 months later demanding $1,093.16 more; resolved historically only via press ([source case 2.2](../initial-research/cases/02-commercial-in-network.md))
**Input:** "My husband passed… bill was sorted, I paid my part $823.15… now 14 months later a NEW bill says I owe $1,093.16 more… do I have to pay this?" + revised-bill PDF (charges $110,666 / allowed $60,349 / original resp. $823.15 PAID / revised resp. $1,916.31 / new balance $1,093.16).
**Transcript:** [_raw/s02-c2-rebilled-after-death.md](_raw/s02-c2-rebilled-after-death.md)

## What happened
- Led with empathy, then nailed the core defect: **a revised bill with no explanation isn't payable on faith**; listed the few legitimate reasons a patient share rises vs. the error scenarios.
- Made the **EOB the deciding document** and gave the exact test: current BCBS EOB still $823.15 → don't pay (likely improper balance-billing on an in-network claim); EOB now $1,916.31 → it may be real, ask why and appeal.
- Handled the collections fear precisely: call to flag account **in dispute**, follow up **in writing**, plus the accurate reassurance that medical debt under $500 doesn't report and there's a one-year reporting delay.
- Surfaced the **estate-vs-guarantor liability** nuance (debt belongs to the estate; guarantor signature varies; probate matters) — a real, non-obvious protection.

## Gaps (vs. ideal advocate)
- The documented real-world resolver was **press/advocate escalation**; the model (appropriately, pre-collections) didn't surface that this class of dispute historically only moved via press — a persistent advocate would keep that in reserve.
- Didn't name **timely-filing rules** that can cap how late a provider re-bills, nor **FDCPA** if it reaches collections.
- Stateless: can't keep the original EOB + paid receipt in an evidence log, which is exactly what this case needs.
