Stage 5 — Verdict · D10 v0.2: primary posture + stacked tracks, deadline-ordered — grounded in practitioner frameworks

D10 · 4-step cascadeVerdict router v0.2(0) Premise check: already paid / wrong patient / identity theft / other payer (COB, workers’ comp, auto) → REJECT. (1) Status gates (stack): in collections → VALIDATE (always stacks) · adjudication incomplete → WAIT. (2) Fights (stack): insurer denied/underpaid → APPEAL · provider high-confidence errors/violations → CONTEST. (3) Affordability sequence: FAP-eligible → REDUCE before any payment (IRS 501(r): retroactive refund right) → billed above commercial band (~175–325% of Medicare) → NEGOTIATE → else PAY. Judgment-only findings never CONTEST alone — negotiation leverage.
RejectNot your billalready paid / wrong patient / other payer → correction or redirect, counterparty varies
WaitPre-adjudicationreminder loop back to Stage 3
ValidateIn collectionsFDCPA §1692g demand — 30-day clock, collection freezes until verified
AppealInsurer deniedcounterparty = insurer: internal appeal (60–180d) → binding external review
ContestProvider errors / violationslevers ranked by $ × win-probability
ReduceCharity-care eligibleFAP before paying — 120d ECA moratorium, 240d application window

PayChecks out, fair pricetrust-building honesty + payment-plan guidance
Stacking ruleVerdicts composeconfirmed real-world stacks: CONTEST+REDUCE · CONTEST+VALIDATE · APPEAL+CONTEST · REDUCE+NEGOTIATE · WAIT+flag. UI shows one primary posture; the action plan orders all stacked tracks by statutory deadline (FDCPA 30d → appeal window → FAP 240d → dispute → negotiate)
